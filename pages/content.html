Designing for Content, A Case Study:

Taylor Wagstaff, 25th July, 2024.

The following website is a record of a chosen topic completed throughout my course provided by Content Design London. I decided to document my work as a handy reminder of the many methods and tools content designers can use to make accessible and user-first digital products.

For my topic, I choose to explore a potential user problem of finding an art studio. I noticed a large gap in the digital space for a single place to locate art studios as there were no effective digital solutions to be found online. What I did find, however, were many facebook groups, some forums and dedicated profiles on Instagram. From my own personal experience, it has largely been through word of mouth that I have managed to find a suitable studio for my own artistic practice. This user problem presented an ideal candidate to explore content design in detail as it covers a wide range of user needs and concerns.

How do users speak?

To find out who my potential users are, I have to understand how my users communicate around the topic. This helps to create a mental model of our users, or rather, what words our users might initially think of around the user problem. One of the main purposes of Content Design is to help our users fulfil a need, which could range from ordering a new TV, to booking a Hotel for your upcoming holiday. In order to fulfil that need, a user normally begins with research on their chosen topic. In this instance however, since I am aiming to create a digital solution, Google will form as a first initial step in the user journey. I have narrowed down from a list of key words which my users may use to search for an art studio online. By using Google Trends, I can provide a way to track the usage of key words over time. This gives me the most commonly used terms regarding my topic.
